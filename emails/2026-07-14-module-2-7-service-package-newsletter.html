Hey there 👋 Alice here. This month I got to build my favorite kind of lesson: no new framework, no theory. We took one real, finished client engagement and reverse-engineered it into something you can actually sell.

Module 2.7: Service Package from a Real Engagement is live, and honestly, it's the lesson I wish someone had handed me two jobs ago. The whole practice is four steps:

1. Mine. Point the agent at your project folder. It reads everything, yes, even the complaint threads, and cites every claim to a file.
2. Decide. You make five calls before anything gets written: target buyer, anonymize or not, which deliverables first.
3. Blueprint. The internal operating manual. A new PM could rerun the whole engagement from this document alone.
4. Derive. Case study, offer sheet, landing page. All quoting one source of truth, so the numbers never drift.

We tested it on a real production-support engagement while writing the lesson, and the numbers it dug up made me sit up: zero SLA breaches across 151 tickets. Resolution rate up from 64% to 87%. Every figure traceable to a monthly report. That's the part I love. Nothing invented, just excavated.
